Core Intelligence Layer

The CIL is the analytical engine behind ClearLine OS. Its four components work together to track, score, and surface the regulatory signals that shape operator decisions.

JSE
Jurisdictional Signal Engine

Reads jurisdictional signals and maps how each state's regulatory environment is structured. Hosts the Statutory Placement Index (SPI) as a sub-component.

EMX
Enforcement Matrix

Tracks and categorizes enforcement behavior across jurisdictions, mapping patterns over time to reveal how agencies actually enforce versus what the statute says.

DIN
Drift Index Number

A numerical score measuring how far a grey area has drifted from clear statutory guidance — helping prioritize which ambiguities deserve attention first.

SSU
Stalwart Suite

The set of landmark cases and laws (LOTLs) that anchor the KB's legal framework. These are the fixed reference points every analysis traces back to.

Statutory Placement Index (SPI)

Five ways a state can place cannabis in its statutory code. The SPI is a classification sub-component of the JSE.

Reg & Pol Playbooks

Five behavioral archetypes for how markets regulate. Type-specific, not state-specific — the same archetype can appear across multiple states.

RNP-01
Mini Muni

Municipal cadence layered on top of state statute. Local rules compound state requirements for operators in these markets.

RNP-02
Wet Paint

Nascent markets still establishing their policies. Rules are in motion and ambiguity is high — early entrants absorb the most regulatory uncertainty.

RNP-03
Headliners

Enforcement as spectacle. High-visibility actions shape operator behavior more than the underlying statute in these markets.

RNP-04
Weathervanes

Enforcement sensitive to political headwinds. The regulatory climate here shifts with election cycles and executive priorities.

RNP-05
Metronomes

Steady, cadence-heavy enforcement. These markets keep consistent rhythm regardless of political climate — predictable but unrelenting.

How the System Flows

The flow is top-down. The CIL sets the analytical foundation. The SPI and RNPs frame how each state is classified. Penumbrant Papers carry the state-specific intelligence — and inside each PEN, Grey Area entries (GAs) are the discrete, numbered intelligence items operators and compliance teams use directly.

  • CIL informs everything — four components, one analytical engine.
  • SPI + RNPs frame the state — statutory placement type and behavioral archetype.
  • PENs carry the intelligence — four sections per state document.
  • GAs are the ground floor — discrete, numbered, actionable.

Each layer is legible on its own, but the value compounds as you move down — context from the top sharpens what the bottom-layer intelligence means in practice.
