## Purpose

Real-world compliance issues encountered in the field, documented as problem-solution pairs. A practical reference for operators facing specific compliance challenges.

---

## Outline

### 1. Format

Each field problem follows a consistent structure:
- **Problem:** Description of the compliance issue as encountered
- **Context:** When/where this typically occurs; license type affected
- **Risk Level:** Low / Medium / High / Critical
- **Resolution:** How to address it (regulatory-compliant approach)
- **Penumbra Note:** How this plays out in enforcement reality (if different from the letter of the law)

### 2. Inventory & Tracking Problems

- METRC discrepancy discovered during internal audit
- Missing transfer manifest for received shipment
- Plant count mismatch between physical and METRC
- Waste event not recorded within required timeframe

### 3. Packaging & Labeling Problems

- Product on shelf with outdated label format
- Missing universal THC symbol on existing inventory
- Potency label doesn't match COA results
- Multi-serving product in non-resealable packaging

### 4. Security & Facility Problems

- Surveillance camera gap discovered (blind spot or equipment failure)
- Visitor entered limited-access area without logging
- Alarm system false positive — documentation requirements
- Premises layout changed without notifying MED

### 5. Employee & Administrative Problems

- Employee working with expired MED badge
- Background check issue discovered after hiring
- Record requested by MED that cannot be located
- Ownership change not reported within required timeframe

### 6. Testing & Product Quality Problems

- Batch fails testing after production costs incurred
- COA discrepancy between testing lab and internal records
- Product complaint from consumer — adverse reaction report
- Recalled product already sold — notification procedures

### 7. Financial & Tax Problems

- Cash handling discrepancy discovered during audit
- Sales tax calculation error across product categories
- Unreported transaction flagged by DOR
