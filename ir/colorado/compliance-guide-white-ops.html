Add current fee schedules, renewal timelines, and common denial reasons from MED data.

Renewal Process

  • Annual renewal required — submit 90 days before expiration
  • Background check updates for all associated key persons
  • Financial responsibility documentation
  • Compliance history review by MED

Common Pitfalls

  • Late renewal submissions triggering temporary license suspension
  • Unreported ownership changes or new financial interest holders
  • Lapsed local permits while state license remains active

4. Operational Compliance

Security Requirements

  • 24/7 video surveillance covering all limited-access areas
  • Minimum 40-day video retention
  • Commercial-grade locks on all entry points
  • Visitor log for all non-badge-holding individuals
  • Alarm system with law enforcement notification

Record-Keeping

  • All transaction records maintained for a minimum of four years
  • Employee records including background check documentation
  • Inventory audit trails reconciled with METRC
  • Financial records available for MED and DOR inspection
Add specific record retention periods per document category and cite relevant rule sections.

Employee Requirements

  • MED-issued Occupational License (badge) required for all employees
  • Badge must be visible during working hours
  • Background check disqualifiers (felony convictions within specified period)

5. METRC & Seed-to-Sale Tracking

Colorado uses METRC (Marijuana Enforcement Tracking Reporting Compliance) as its mandatory seed-to-sale tracking system.

Core Obligations

  • Tag every plant, package, and transfer in METRC
  • Reconcile physical inventory with METRC records daily
  • Report all waste/destruction events within 24 hours
  • Maintain transfer manifests for every product movement

Common METRC Violations

Violation Severity Typical Consequence
Inventory discrepancies exceeding threshold High Investigation, potential administrative action
Late or missing transfer manifests Medium Written warning, escalating fines
Untagged plants or packages High Mandatory quarantine, potential license action
Failure to report waste events Medium Compliance notice, audit trigger

6. Packaging & Labeling Requirements

Mandatory Label Elements

  • Universal THC symbol
  • Potency statement (THC/CBD content in mg)
  • Net weight / volume
  • License number
  • Batch / lot number traceable in METRC
  • Standardized warning statements
  • Ingredient list (for infused products)

Packaging Standards

  • Child-resistant packaging (ASTM D3475 or 16 CFR 1700)
  • Opaque, non-see-through containers for retail product
  • No packaging that appeals to minors (cartoon characters, candy mimicry)
  • Re-sealable if multi-serving
Add current MED-approved universal symbol specifications and any recent labeling rule changes.

7. Testing & Quality Assurance

Mandatory Testing Categories

  • Potency — THC and CBD quantification
  • Residual solvents — For concentrate / extract products
  • Microbial contaminants — Salmonella, E. coli, Aspergillus
  • Pesticides — Full panel per MED-approved list
  • Heavy metals — Lead, mercury, arsenic, cadmium
  • Mycotoxins — Aflatoxins, ochratoxin A

Testing Workflow

  1. Batch created and tagged in METRC
  2. Representative sample collected by licensed testing facility
  3. Results uploaded to METRC; batch passes or fails
  4. Failed batches: remediation pathway or destruction

8. Enforcement Patterns & the Penumbra

This section covers ClearLine's proprietary analysis of how Colorado regulations are actually enforced — the gap between what the rules say and what triggers real enforcement action.

Enforcement Priorities (Observed)

  • Diversion indicators — Inventory discrepancies suggesting product leaving the regulated supply chain are the top enforcement trigger
  • Financial irregularities — Unexplained cash flow patterns, unreported ownership interests
  • Packaging / marketing to minors — Politically sensitive; enforcement is swift
  • METRC compliance — Systemic tracking failures escalate faster than isolated incidents

Lower-Priority Areas (Observed)

  • Minor record-keeping formatting issues (typically result in corrective notice)
  • First-time, low-magnitude labeling deviations
  • Isolated METRC data entry errors corrected within 72 hours

10. Action Checklist

A prioritized checklist for licensed Colorado operators to assess and maintain compliance.

Critical (Address Immediately)

  • ☐ METRC inventory reconciled and current (no open discrepancies)
  • ☐ All employees hold valid MED Occupational Licenses
  • ☐ Video surveillance operational and meeting retention requirements
  • ☐ Current license displayed at premises; renewal timeline tracked

High Priority (Address Within 30 Days)

  • ☐ Packaging and labeling reviewed against current MED requirements
  • ☐ All product testing current and results in METRC
  • ☐ Security plan updated and consistent with actual operations
  • ☐ Financial records organized for potential DOR/MED review

Ongoing Maintenance

  • ☐ Monthly internal compliance audit (self-assessment)
  • ☐ Track MED rulemaking notices for regulatory changes
  • ☐ Review local ordinance changes in operating municipality
  • ☐ Annual training refresh for all staff on compliance procedures
